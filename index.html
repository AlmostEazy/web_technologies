Whalehead, or the royal heron!

About

A species of birds from the order Pelicanidae, the only representative of the whale-headed family (Balaenicipitidae). A very large bird, its average height is 1.2 m, wingspan is 2.3 m, and weight is from 4 to 7 kg. It lives in the tropical swamps of East Africa.
Unlike most other birds, the whale's eyes are located on the front of the skull, not on the sides, which allows it to have binocular vision. Due to the massiveness of the beak, the bird puts it on its chest during rest or hunting.

Food

The whalehead feeds during the day. He diligently examines the floating islands of aquatic plants. Usually the whale-head feeds on fish, mainly protoptera, tilapia and catfish, it also catches frogs, snakes and even young turtles and crocodiles. During hunting, this bird is distinguished by great patience. Motionless, with her head down, she patiently waits in the thickets of reeds when prey appears not far away.

Distribution

The whalehead inhabits the entire upper basin of the Nile and its numerous tributaries, in the reservoirs of the Rift Valley, the westernmost and southernmost, up to Tanzania, Zambia, including, it is found in the area of South Sudan, Ethiopia, Uganda, in the republics of Central Africa, Rwanda, the Democratic Republic of the Congo. The territory forms a strip with a north-south direction, evenly divided by an equatorial line. A sedentary bird, usually spends its whole life in the area where it was born.
